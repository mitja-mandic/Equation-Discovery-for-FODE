# Connections with existing methods

The paper places penalized LDA in conversation with two especially relevant methods.

- sparse discriminant analysis
- nearest shrunken centroids

These connections are useful because they show that the new proposal is not an isolated idea. It sits in
a broader family of high-dimensional classification methods that trade off density, stability, and
interpretability in different ways.

## The practical comparison

The point of the comparison is not to declare a universal winner. It is to clarify what penalized LDA
adds:

- it preserves the Fisher perspective
- it gives a direct handle on the discriminant vectors
- it can express both sparsity and ordered structure through the chosen penalty

This chapter is where a reader should come away understanding how the method differs from other sparse
LDA-style approaches in both formulation and intent.
