# Discussion and reading notes

The paper ends by emphasizing a simple but durable idea: in high-dimensional classification, it is often
better to regularize the discriminant directions themselves than to force a classical method to work
unchanged.

That is the real message to carry forward.

## What to retain

- Classical LDA is elegant, but the high-dimensional regime changes the problem.
- Penalization can make Fisher’s discriminant approach usable and interpretable.
- Optimization matters, because a good idea is not useful unless it can be solved reliably.

## Reading note

If you want to revisit the original technical details later, the most useful order is:

1. the Fisher formulation in Chapter 2
2. the optimization idea in Chapter 3
3. the penalized proposal in Chapter 4
4. the comparisons in Chapters 6 and 7

That order matches the logic of the paper, but it is easier to remember than the raw article structure.
